Rising Echoes

Curated by Hiloni Patel

This exhibition features works by artists who use musical elements to enhance the viewer's experience, by exploring beyond the limits of web designs. The visuals incorporated in these projects are aesthetically pleasing, as the artists use unconventional graphics to express their desired message for the art piece. Each piece explores key themes such as users, relationships, cultural history, musical expression, and feminism. Most of the works were created between the late 1990s to early 2000s, a period that influenced Cyber-Feminine Club which plays a pivotal role in the project Station Radio, Season 4's. This era also highlights how an artist's ability to explore beyond the web's potential led to technological advances that continue to shape the digital world today. By creating online musical projects, artists are able to reach diverse audiences, using music, a universal language, to take users on an emotional journey. Such projects offer artists endless creative freedom to evolve with the trends of the society and express themselves in innovative ways.

Title: One Night in Greenwich Village
Year: 1998
Artist: Jeannette Lambert

One Night in Greenwich Village is a streaming audio web art project that features sound gathered on the streets of New York City. The artist experiments with invisible sound files to allow users to experience living in the Greenwich Village neighborhood. It's also an unconventional form of storytelling, as audio and graphic images represent the neighborhood's cultural history.

Title: Vocabulary
Year: 1996-2003
Artist: X Baczewska

Vocabulary is an original vocal composition with access through a musical component, graphics, and user interaction. The musical element of the work is an audio version of a short piece vocally performed by the artist, X Baczewska himself. The graphical component is the collage of words comprising the piece's lyrics. The interactive element is the user's ability to compose the piece in their voice by clicking the words of the lyrics in any order.

Title: Station Radio. Season 4
Year: 2020
Artist: Anna Soz

Station Radio, Season 4, is the fourth season of occasional broadcast, Station Radio, that features audio work from independent musicians that explore particular themes. Season 4 features music from artists who explore their feminist art, which connects the past and present through harmony and technology. It explores the archives of Cyber-Femin-Club and uses Anna Soz's drawings and video to highlight the theme of the tracks. It features tracks from non-mainstream artists like Ushko and Sofia Rodina.

Title: The Sound of Love
Year: 2022
Artist: Chia Amisola

The Sound of Love is a website created by Chia Amisola featuring comments from love songs on Youtube. The comments display people's emotional connection with the song and how music embarks them on a journey. It also explores how the anonymity of a comment section allows people to freely express themselves in ways that would have been difficult in real life.
